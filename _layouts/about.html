Hey, I'm Dan!

I'm a front end web developer, and I love what I do!

my story.

My interest in computers and programming stems from back in 2013, when a friend of mine introduced me to coding. I used visual basic to develop a few simple apps, including a guitar chord picker and a tool to help me with my current A-level revision. Doing this really opened my eyes to how fun coding can be, and the abilities that you gain just by having simple knowledge.

Around the same time as this, I built my own computer in the switch from console to PC gaming, and without sounding too crazy, I'd say it was one of the most fun things I've ever done - making all of the pieces fit together, troubleshooting, and then ending up with a great gaming computer for a fraction of the price of a pre-built one was a great feeling, and I wanted MORE!

Ultimately this lead to me changing my aspirational career path from an accountant/economist/teacher (being 17 is a very indecisive time!) to become someone that does something to do with computers. So, from there I applied for multiple courses across the country from game development to computer science, and ended up happily in my hometown doing computer systems engineering at the University of Birmingham.

My degree covered many areas, from logic, assembly and microprocessors to using C and Java to develop our own programs. I gained valuable experience in hard skills and soft skills, with management and group projects which included building a robot that followed a line along the floor.

I graduated from the University of Birmingham with a 2:1 in Computer Systems Engineering in July 2017. From there, I decided to take a break from education and spent the following year travelling around Australia with my girlfriend whilst she took part in a year abroad for her psychology degree.

Towards the end of my time away, I started applying for various jobs and graduate schemes for when I got back. I reached the interview stage for a few of these, but quickly realised that the skills that I gained from my degree would not be enough on their own to land me a job in development.

So, while working at the student accomodation at the university to keep me going, I started looking for technologies that I would both enjoy, and would give me plenty of choice when looking for a job.

I went through many technologies, many of which felt very pointless, without something to be aiming for.

Eventually I found freecodecamp and started their tutorials on HTML and CSS. I found the ability to be able to see something tangible being made very addictive, and the idea that I could create me very own website that people could look at was very exciting.

I completed the basic projects, and then started learning JavaScript, and that's when I became thirsty. I was thirsty to learn new things, and it felt like front-end web development had almost too many things that I needed to learn.

From there, I have completed a few projects for co-workers and friends, from static sites to wordpress. My job search also came to a happy end, being accepted into a software firm in Nottingham that uses Angular to create HR solutions as a single page application.
